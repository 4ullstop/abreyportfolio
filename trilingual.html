Pre-Production

A prototype was developed in one week drawing inspiration from Chants of Sennaar's mechanics and gameplay loop. Initial player experience was frustrating due to the lack of direction and feedback. Despite this, the core intent was one that provided players with profound epiphany. This would be the guide for subsequent design decisions throughout development.

The Paper Process

I began all design by drawing out ideas on paper, whether for systems, levels, or mechanics. This kept the design process on track and prevented distractions. This also allowed for easier implementation during the creation process.

A simplified prototyped version of the various levels of communication to achieve our rune scanning and guessing system.

Paper prototyping was also used during early brainstorming sessions. I would write down various ideas for puzzle mechanics pushing the promising ones to more brainstorming time and eliminating ideas that were lacking.

Mechanic Development

Balancing the emphasis on deciphering alien text while introducing new and engaging player interactions was a significant challenge during this phase of development. We realized we needed to be cautious in creating new mechanics so that they would not overshadow the game's core loop. My approach to solving this problem invovled the development of a tile-flipping system which enabled players to "draw" the rune through a mouse click based on the context clues in the environment. These context clues would also aid in the understanding of the rune's definition.

Throughout its development, I prioritized simplifying the implementation of the mechanic. This allowed me to concentrate on enhancing the player's learning experience within the mechanic's rules.

This system was implemented through a multi dimensional struct with an XY coordinate system. You simply plug in the X and Y locations of the correct tiles with the rest of the incorrect tiles filling in automatically.

Tiles spawned based on coordinates, and each was programmed to recognize correctness. When clicked the "manager" blueprint would record the location of the tile and, after reaching a limit, evaluated if all correct tiles were flipped, providing feedback accordingly.

Core Loop Mechanic Development

Decoding starts in the player. Here it's recognized if the item scanned was a rune.

I utilized the gamemode to be the main brain of the operation. Once scanned, the run reports it's value and transmits it's data to the gamemode.

The gamemode would then report the proper information to the HUD where the menu for guessing would appear.

After inputting a guess, it's sent back to the gamemode for proper evaluation which would result in further communications depending on this result. This flow of information allowed for easy implementation of the new runes which was crucial to the development given the development length.
