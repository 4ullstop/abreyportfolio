Potion Seller

  • Platform: PC
  • Software: Unreal Engine, Github, Trello
  • Creation Length: 6 months
  • Playtime: 10 minutes

Potion Seller is a 2.5D side scroller where you play as the often over-looked, side character shop-keep the Potion Seller. As the only townsperson willing to stand agains the demon enemies, you must fight your way with potion-based combat to defeat the final boss known as "The General".

Responsibilities

  • Design and scripted enemy AI.
  • Created and assigned team member's tasks.
  • Designed and implemented a versatile camera system.
  • Designed and scripted multiple potion types for players to use.

Ice Spike Potion

Among many of the mechanics I created, the one I'm choosing to highlight here was one of the more difficult. According to our design, the potion needed to not only align with the ground but with any surface of varying degree.

In order to produce the result you see above, I utilized multiple line traces. The first of which casts horizontally trying to determine if the ground in front of it is angled. If it is, then it spawns the next spike at the hit location taking the normal of the surface into account.

The second line trace only occurs if the first trace didn't hit, in which case this one casts vertically to the ground to determine its location to spawn a new spike.

Camera System

Another system I was in charge of was our camera system. This system worked by constantly getting the player's location and following it along the x and z-axis. To make it feel more juicy, camera lag was introduced via interping the current location to the last location.

To make the system more customizable for our level designers, items were exposed to the details panel such as distance from the player, height and lag speed.

We also discovered the need for the ability for the camera to be locked depending on combat encounters and so the system was adjusted as so. This is simply triggered by the player walking into the box and can be set back to the regular camera by having the player walk out of the box.

Enemies

This is a demonstration of the Demon Bird enemy I helped design and was in charge of developing. This enemy proved to be difficult with it being in a perpetual state of flying. According to our documentation, the bird required two states.

The first of which was a mid air patrolling. In this state, the bird flys between two target points.

The next state was the attacking state which it entered when the player is in sight of the bird. In this state, it dives down to the player to cause damage only to return to a temporary patrolling state in order to give the player a break between its attacks.

This ground dwelling enemy with a long sphere is another enemy I helped design and was tasked with developing. Similar to the Demon Bird, the Goblin also had two states, one being patrolling and the other being attacking.

During this patrolling state, the Goblin travels between two set patrol points.

As for its attacking state, the Goblin rushes at the player only to quickly retreat a short distance from them. This behavior continues until either it or the player is dead as it will continue to chase the player. This retreat exists for the same reason it did in the Demon Bird, to give the player a break between attacks as we learned through playtesting the detrimental results of a continuous attack from an enemy.

Patrol System

I was also in charge of designing and developing the patrol system for the enemies enabling them to patrol between multiple target points.

Boss State

All enemies were also equipped with a boss state which I developed for the purposes of allowing enemies to constantly charge at the player. This state is used during the fight with The General as one of the waves of attacks The General has.
